Welcome

Welcome to the Peake Honors Program! We are comprised of a diverse group of 35-40 students from all different majors. Our goal is to provide academically-motivated students—like yourself!—with further opportunities for engagement and growth. To that end, we offer interdisciplinary courses, independent research, and enriching out-of-class activities. Perhaps most importantly, you get to explore these opportunities with a group of like-minded peers that you can interact with both socially and intellectually. Being a member of the Honors Program will both enhance your time at UVA Wise and also position you to do great things after you graduate. Please explore the above tabs to learn more.







Trips

Each year, the Program offers a number of off-campus excursions that encourage members to experience and engage with the wider world. In past semesters, we have taken day trips to local destinations like the:

  • Carter Fold
  • Gray Fossil Site
  • National Storytelling Festival
  • Bristol Caverns

In addition, we typically take one overnight trip per semester. This allows us to range further afield, visit a larger city, and sample its cultural amenities (plays, concerts, historical sites, museums, ethnic food, etc.). Past destinations include:

  • Charleston, SC
  • Asheville, NC
  • Savannah, GA
  • Williamsburg, VA
  • Louisville, KY
  • Atlanta, GA
  • The Outer Banks, NC

Moreover, all of these trips are free to program members!

Research

Honors students also get to conduct independent research with faculty mentors. Some students complete a Senior Capstone project. Since it cultivates research skills and functions as something of a mini-thesis, the Capstone is a great option for students who are considering graduate school. Recent capstone topics include:


  • The role of women in contemporary terrorist organizations
  • An oral history of Appalachian moonshining
  • Heavy metal as political commentary
  • The suitability of different metal complexes for delivering cancer treatment
  • Covert rhetorics of independence in antebellum black spirituals
  • Neurological responses to religious imagery
  • Mitigating the effects of depression in freshmen college students

Other students choose two smaller alternative research projects. This option gives students training in conducting less traditional and more creative research projects. Past students have:

  • Kept a reflective journal during a study abroad experience in Dortmund, Germany
  • Examined issues of local water quality through the lens of environmental ethics
  • Written and illustrated a graphic novella
  • Gathered data on light pollution in Utah and Arizona national parks
  • Composed a series of lyrical essays that explore mental health disorders (e.g. bipolar, OCD, depression) as they manifest themselves in daily life.

How To Apply

Academic qualifications: Incoming freshmen are required to have a 3.5 high school GPA and a combined SAT score of around 1200. Transfers and current UVA Wise students need a 3.5 college GPA.

Application process: All applicants must submit the application form and the accompanying two-page essay.
In addition, transfers and current UVA Wise students should provide a letter of recommendation from a faculty member (either at UVA Wise or their current school) who is familiar with their academic work.

Timelines: Letters to qualifying high school seniors generally go out in early spring. The late spring application deadline is specified in the letter. Transfers and current students can apply at any time in order to join the following semester.
